Fox

The Fullhop Factory
3 Rules for the Matchup:
  1. Take note of how the Fox reacts to lasers
  2. SHL for defense, use for offense after #1 has been followed
  3. Try to end combos with Fox offstage


The 3 rules in-depth:
1. Take note of how the Fox is responding to lasers. Are they immediately fullhoppping after laser stun and coming down with an aerial? Are they dash-upsmashing? Turn around uptilt? Dashback pivot grab? Powershield? Once you've deciphered your opponents tendencies around lasers, you can start approaching with options that beat those tendencies.
2. As mentioned above, Fox has a myriad of options to deal with lasers. Try to approach after a laser only when you feel confident in your read on how Fox will respond to that laser. If you're wrong, your approach turns into a punish for your opponent. Until you feel confident, use lasers to limit Fox's approaches and to force your opponent how they will deal with your lasers.
3. Get your easy 40% and prioritize getting them offstage. Overextending combos can result in missed edgeguard opportunities and potentially turn into a punish for your opponent. You don't always need to get combo video material! Remember, spacies always have a chance of dying when they're put offstage.

SDIing Thunder's Combo

Letting Fox convert into a grab or upsmash after he shines is giving away free damage. Getting knocked down by an aerial shine and getting jab reset shouldn't have to happen if you're ready for it.

Counterplay

Once you've been hit by an aerial and Fox chooses to shine after, you can react to the shine happening and hold up to pop out of the jab and get your own opening. Depending on what the Fox is choosing after the jab reset, different options you choose can be better or worse. Try to avoid panic spotdodging after you SDI since its timing is about the same as getting jab reset.
